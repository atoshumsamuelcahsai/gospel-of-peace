The Apostles' Creed

Our Statement of Faith

The Apostles' Creed is a statement of Christian faith that has been used by believers for centuries.

The Apostles' Creed

I believe in God, the Father almighty,
creator of heaven and earth.

I believe in Jesus Christ, his only Son, our Lord,
who was conceived by the Holy Spirit,
born of the Virgin Mary,
suffered under Pontius Pilate,
was crucified, died, and was buried;
he descended to the dead.
On the third day he rose again;
he ascended into heaven,
he is seated at the right hand of the Father,
and he will come to judge the living and the dead.

I believe in the Holy Spirit,
the holy catholic Church,
the communion of saints,
the forgiveness of sins,
the resurrection of the body,
and the life everlasting.

Amen.

What We Believe

The Apostles' Creed summarizes the essential beliefs of Christianity. It affirms our faith in the Trinity - Father, Son, and Holy Spirit - and the core events of Jesus' life, death, and resurrection.

Our Foundation

This creed has been recited by Christians for over 1,500 years. It serves as a foundation for our faith and a reminder of the essential truths we hold dear as followers of Christ.
